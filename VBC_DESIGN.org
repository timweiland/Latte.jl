#+TITLE: A Variational Bayes Correction (VBC) Mode for Latte's INLA
#+AUTHOR: Latte design note
#+OPTIONS: toc:2 num:nil
#+STARTUP: showall

* Motivation

Benchmarking the Paraná §2.8 model (Gamma + rw1(seaDist) + Matérn-SPDE) against
R-INLA surfaced a structural gap: Latte's *augmented* INLA formulation (the linear
predictors carried as explicit latent nodes, pinned by a \(\sim 10^6\) precision
penalty) costs roughly *2x* end-to-end and produces a posterior precision with
condition number \(\sim 10^{13}\), which also *blocks safe warm-starting* of the
mode-finder. A controlled 2x2 experiment confirmed it: holding the marginalization
fixed, dropping the augmentation (the "compact" formulation, already reachable via
~parana_model(...; augment=false)~) is a clean ~2x speedup at identical accuracy,
landing *below* R-INLA's warm time.

The catch is that the augmentation buys a *diagonal likelihood*, which is what
makes the cheap per-node Simplified Laplace (SLA) skew correction work. Drop the
augmentation and the per-node SLA no longer applies; the base marginals become
Gaussian-on-the-mode, and the Laplace *mean* (= the mode) is biased for skewed
conditionals. The fix — the same one the INLA authors adopted for their "compact"
mode — is a *low-rank Variational Bayes correction (VBC)* that cheaply recovers the
posterior-mean accuracy without re-introducing the augmentation.

This document (1) explains VBC and the compact reformulation assuming familiarity
with Latte's current INLA, (2) designs a VBC mode that coexists with the existing
augmented mode (VBC the new default), with efficiency at the centre, and (3) lays
out how to evaluate the prototype.

* Part I --- Explanation

** Why a Variational Bayes Correction: theory and the compact reformulation

This section is the conceptual core of the design. It assumes you already
understand Latte's *current* INLA: the augmented Laplace approximation (latent
field \(=\) \([\eta; x_{\text{base}}]\) with \(\eta \approx A x_{\text{base}}\)
pinned by a \(\sim 10^6\) precision penalty), the per-node Simplified Laplace
(SLA) skew correction, and the hyperparameter loop that mode-finds \(\theta\),
explores a grid \(\{\theta_k\}\), and \(\theta\)-mixes the per-\(\theta\)
marginals. The goal here is to teach *why* we want the Variational Bayes
Correction (VBC), *why* the compact (un-augmented) reformulation forces us to
have it, the VBC math step by step, and how it stays cheap.

*** The fundamental problem: Laplace gives you the MODE, but you want the MEAN

INLA's per-\(\theta\) inner approximation is a Gaussian
\(\pi_G(x \mid \theta, y) = \mathcal N(\mu(\theta), Q_X(\theta)^{-1})\) built by a
second-order Taylor expansion of the log-conditional \(\log \pi(x\mid\theta,y)\)
about its *mode* \(\mu(\theta)\). By construction this is the negative-Hessian
quadratic centered at the mode:

\[
\log \pi(x\mid\theta,y) \;\approx\; \text{const} \;-\; \tfrac12 (x-\mu)^\top Q_X (x-\mu).
\]

A Gaussian is symmetric, so its mean *equals* its mode. But the *true*
conditional \(\pi(x\mid\theta,y)\) is generally **skewed** whenever the
likelihood is non-Gaussian (low-count Poisson, Gamma, Bernoulli — the
log-likelihood in \(\eta\)-space is asymmetric). For a right-skewed density the
mean sits away from the mode, pushed toward the heavy tail. So the Gaussian
approximation, anchored at the mode, reports the *wrong location*: it reports
\(\mu = \text{mode}\) when the quantity a Bayesian actually wants for a point
summary and for downstream integrals is \(\mathbb E[x\mid\theta,y] = \text{mean}\).

#+begin_quote
*Intuition (cumulant view).* Think of the error of the Gaussian approximation
order by order. The *mean* (1st cumulant) is wrong at *first order* in the
skewness — it is the leading, cheapest-to-fix error. The *variance* (2nd
cumulant) is only wrong at *higher order*; the negative Hessian \(Q_X^{-1}\)
already captures the local curvature well. So the principled, parsimonious move
is: **trust the Hessian variance, fix only the location.** This is exactly what
VBC does — keep \(Q_X\) fixed, correct only the mean. It is also exactly the
division of labor SLA already follows, as we see next.
#+end_quote

*** How this relates to what SLA already does

Latte's current SLA is itself a mode\(\to\)mean+skew correction, just done
per-node. For each latent index \(i\) it forms a skew-normal from a third-order
Taylor expansion of the Laplace marginal, parameterized by two cumulant-style
quantities (see ~src/laplace/simplified_laplace.jl~, \(\gamma_1, \gamma_3\) in
~_get_skew_params~):

- \(\gamma_1\) — a *first-order location* correction (shifts the marginal mean
  off the mode), and
- \(\gamma_3\) — a *third-order skewness* term (bends the marginal into a
  skew-normal).

Note the marginal *standard deviation* feeding both comes straight from the GA's
selected-inverse diagonal \(\sigma_i = \sqrt{(Q_X^{-1})_{ii}}\) and is **not**
re-derived — same "trust the Hessian variance" philosophy. So SLA = (Hessian
variance) + (location fix \(\gamma_1\)) + (skew \(\gamma_3\)).

VBC keeps the first two of those three and drops the third *from the conditional*:

| Ingredient            | SLA (current)                  | VBC (proposed, compact)                 |
|-----------------------+--------------------------------+-----------------------------------------|
| Conditional variance  | Hessian \(\sigma_i\) (kept)    | Hessian \(\sigma_i\) (kept, unchanged)  |
| Conditional location  | per-node \(\gamma_1\)          | *joint* low-rank \(\mu^* = \mu + M\lambda\) |
| Conditional skew      | per-node \(\gamma_3\)          | none (conditional stays Gaussian)       |
| Marginal skew         | conditional \(\gamma_3\) + \(\theta\)-mixture | \(\theta\)-mixture *only* |

The key structural difference: SLA's location fix is *element-wise and local*
(it touches each marginal independently). VBC's location fix is *joint and
propagated* — it nudges a handful of influential nodes and lets the GA precision
matrix spread that correction to the whole field with a guarantee of joint
improvement. We will see why that distinction is exactly what the compact model
needs.

*** The compact reformulation, and why it FORCES VBC

**** The two formulations

The *augmented* (classic) latent field carries the linear predictors as explicit
nodes: \(X = [\eta;\, x_{\text{base}}]\) with the soft constraint
\(\eta \approx A x_{\text{base}}\) enforced by a precision \(Q_\eta = \tau I\),
\(\tau \sim 10^6\). The single structural payoff of this is a **diagonal
likelihood**: datum \(i\) depends on exactly *one* latent element \(\eta_i\). That
conditional independence is precisely what makes the per-node SLA skew correction
both cheap and uniform across components.

The *compact* ("modern") latent field is base components only:
\[
\psi = x_{\text{base}} = \{\beta_0, \beta, f\}, \qquad \psi\mid\theta \sim \mathcal N(0, Q(\theta)^{-1}),
\]
and the predictors are *not* latent — they are the linear map \(\eta = A\psi\).
The joint becomes
\[
\pi(\psi,\theta\mid y) \;\propto\; \pi(\theta)\,\pi(\psi\mid\theta)\,\prod_i \pi\!\big(y_i \mid (A\psi)_i,\, \theta\big).
\]
Each datum now depends on a *row* of \(A\), i.e. on a linear combination of base
nodes — the likelihood is **no longer diagonal in \(\psi\)-coordinates**. The GA
collapses cleanly to the compact Gaussian (this is what Latte already builds with
~augment=false~; see ~LinearlyTransformedLikelihood~):
\[
\log \pi(\psi\mid\theta,y) \approx -\tfrac12 \psi^\top \big(\underbrace{Q(\theta) + A^\top D A}_{Q_X(\theta)}\big)\psi - b^\top A\psi,
\qquad \psi\mid\theta,y \sim \mathcal N(\mu(\theta), Q_X(\theta)^{-1}),
\]
with \(D = \operatorname{diag}\{c_i\}\) the per-observation likelihood curvature
in \(\eta\)-space and \(b\) the gradient, both iterated to the mode. The GA
Hessian \(Q_X = Q + A^\top D A\) is of size \(m\) (base dim), never \(m+n\), and
crucially has **no near-singular \(10^6\) jitter block** (no cond\(\sim 10^{13}\),
no 1049-dim blow-up), which is what makes it faster *and* safe to warm-start.

**** Why dropping \(\eta\) removes the mean accuracy — and why VBC is the fix

Here is the logical chain that makes VBC mandatory, not optional, in compact mode:

1. *Drop the augmented \(\eta\) nodes* \(\Rightarrow\) the likelihood is no longer
   diagonal in latent coordinates.
2. \(\Rightarrow\) the cheap, uniform *per-node SLA* — whose whole premise was
   "each datum touches one latent node" — no longer applies to the components
   that matter (the predictors are gone; the base nodes each see a *sum* of
   likelihood terms).
3. \(\Rightarrow\) the per-\(\theta\) conditional marginals are now **Gaussian on
   the mode** (eq. 11: \(x_j\mid\theta,y \sim \mathcal N(\mu_j(\theta),
   (Q_X^{-1})_{jj})\)). All non-Gaussianity of the final marginal now comes
   *only* from the \(\theta\)-mixture (eq. 12).
4. \(\Rightarrow\) but the *mode* \(\mu(\theta)\) is biased for the *mean*
   whenever the likelihood is skewed (§"the fundamental problem"). Without the
   SLA's \(\gamma_1\) location fix, that bias is now uncorrected. The predictor
   marginals \(\eta = A\psi\) inherit it, and (the authors' words) "the tiny
   errors compound."
5. \(\Rightarrow\) we must **recover the conditional MEAN cheaply**, without
   re-augmenting and without the per-node SLA machinery. *That recovery is VBC.*

The literature considered two rejected alternatives before VBC: (i) plain
Gaussian post-inference of the predictors — cheap but inaccurate, errors compound;
(ii) skew-normal predictor marginals with a Gaussian copula — accurate but it
"essentially negates the computational benefit of removing the linear
predictors." VBC is the third way: it restores *mean* accuracy at the cost of one
extra \(p\)-dimensional solve per \(\theta\). Note the word *mean*: VBC recovers the
location the dropped SLA used to supply (\(\gamma_1\)), but **not** the *conditional
skew* (\(\gamma_3\)) — the conditional stays Gaussian and all marginal skew now
comes from the \(\theta\)-mixture. That is the right trade for the data-rich regime
VBC targets (near-Gaussian conditionals), but a real limitation at fixed /
sharply-concentrated \(\theta\) with a strongly non-Gaussian likelihood — see
"Scope, limitations & mode choice" in the Design for when to reach for the
augmented (or, later, compact+SLA) mode instead.

#+begin_quote
*Intuition.* The augmented model and VBC are two places to pay for getting the
*mean* right under a skewed likelihood. The augmented model pays *up front and
globally*: it inflates the latent field by \(n\) near-singular nodes so a uniform
per-node skew correction becomes possible — and that correction also yields the
conditional *skew* as a byproduct. VBC pays *late and locally*: it keeps the small
well-conditioned field and adds a tiny rank-\(p\) mean nudge per \(\theta\) — but
buys only the mean, not the conditional skew. For data-rich models (data \(\gg\)
model size) with near-Gaussian conditionals the augmentation cost dominates *and*
the skew it would add is negligible, so VBC is the better trade; when the
conditional skew is genuinely large, it is not.
#+end_quote

*** The VBC math, step by step

We now derive VBC for a single fixed \(\theta\) (it runs once per grid point;
see efficiency section). Drop the \(\theta\) argument; write the GA precision as
\(Q_0 := Q_X(\theta) = Q + A^\top D A\) and the GA mode as \(\psi_0 := \mu(\theta)\).

**** Step 1 — the mode is a linear-system solution

At convergence of the GA's Newton iteration, fix
\(Q_0 = H|_{\psi_0}\) (negative Hessian of the log-posterior) and
\(b_0 = \gamma|_{\psi_0} + H|_{\psi_0}\psi_0\) (with \(\gamma = \nabla \log\pi\)).
The mode solves the linear system
\[
Q_0 \,\psi_0 = b_0 . \tag{5}
\]
This reframing — "the mode is the RHS-determined solution of \(Q_0\psi = b_0\)"
— is what lets us correct the mean *implicitly*, by perturbing the RHS.

**** Step 2 — implicit RHS correction instead of explicit mean shift

The naive correction is explicit: replace \(\psi_0\) by \(\psi_1 = \psi_0 +
\delta\) and optimize the *full \(m\)-vector* \(\delta\). That is \(m\)-dimensional
— hopeless for spatial/large random-effect models.

Instead, perturb the RHS of the linear system by a vector \(\lambda\):
\[
Q_0 \,\psi_1 = b_0 + \lambda \;=\; b_1, \qquad \psi_1 = Q_0^{-1} b_1 . \tag{10}
\]
The sensitivity of the solution to a single RHS coordinate is one full column of
the *inverse*:
\[
\frac{\partial \psi_1}{\partial \lambda_j} = Q_0^{\cdot j} \quad\text{(the $j$-th column of $Q_0^{-1}$).} \tag{11–12}
\]

#+begin_quote
*Intuition (why a few nudges fix the whole field).* \(Q_0\) is *sparse* but its
inverse column \(Q_0^{\cdot j}\) is generally *dense*. So injecting a correction
at one RHS coordinate \(\lambda_j\) moves *every* component of \(\psi_1\) along
that dense inverse-column. The conditional-dependence graph baked into \(Q_0\)
propagates a correction made at a few influential "hub" nodes out to all
connected nodes — i.e. to the entire latent field. This is the structural reason
a *rank-\(p\)* nudge can fix an \(m\)-dimensional mean.
#+end_quote

**** Step 3 — the low-rank restriction

Choose an index set \(I \subset \{1,\dots,m\}\) with \(|I| = p \ll m\) — the *hub
coordinates*. Force \(\lambda\) to be supported only on \(I\). Then
\[
\boxed{\;\psi_1 = \psi_0 + Q_{0,I}^{-1}\,\lambda\;}
\]
where \(Q_{0,I}^{-1}\) is the \(m\times p\) matrix of the \(p\) columns of \(Q_0^{-1}\)
indexed by \(I\) (the **propagation matrix** \(M := Q_{0,I}^{-1}\)). Only these
\(p\) columns ever need to be computed — each is one sparse solve \(Q_0 x = e_j\)
against the *existing* GA factorization. The unknown is now the \(p\)-vector
\(\lambda\).

#+begin_quote
*Which nodes go in \(I\)?* Theory says *any* reasonable \(I\) yields a similarly
improved *joint* mean, precisely because the correction propagates through the
single shared GA rather than being applied element-wise — you are "assured of a
joint improvement." Practically: pick the nodes that are (i) most influential and
(ii) connected to many data points. Concretely **all fixed effects \(\beta\)** (each
enters every \(\eta_i\), so they touch all data) plus **"short" random effects**
(small-dimension components replicated across many observations). In Latte these
are readable from ~model.latent_layout~ / the latent groups: fixed effects + short
random-effect blocks.
#+end_quote

**** Step 4 — the variational objective for \(\lambda\)

We pick \(\lambda\) by a variational principle (Zellner's optimal
information-processing rule), which — unlike a plain ELBO — does *not* require the
unknown true posterior. Among Gaussians \(g\), choose the one minimizing
"expected \(-\)loglik plus KL-to-prior":
\[
\lambda^* = \arg\min_{\lambda}\;
\underbrace{\mathbb E_{\psi \sim \mathcal N(\psi_0 + M\lambda,\, Q_0^{-1})}\big[-\log \pi(y\mid\psi)\big]}_{\text{expected data misfit}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{\operatorname{KLD}\!\big(\mathcal N(\psi_0+M\lambda, Q_0^{-1})\,\big\|\,\pi(\psi)\big)}_{\text{stay close to prior}} . \tag{13}
\]
Interpretation: among all Gaussians with the *fixed* GA covariance \(Q_0^{-1}\),
shift the mean to best fit the data while staying anchored to the prior. The
final answer is \(\psi_1 = \psi_0 + M\lambda^*\), precision unchanged at \(Q_0\).

For an LGM the prior is Gaussian \(\mathcal N(0, Q_\pi^{-1})\) and \(q\) shares the
same covariance \(Q_0^{-1}\), so the KL between them keeps only the Mahalanobis
term (the log-det and trace terms *cancel* — no determinant is ever computed):
\[
\operatorname{KLD} \;\to\; \tfrac12\,(\psi_0 + M\lambda)^\top\, Q_\pi\, (\psi_0 + M\lambda) \;+\; \text{const}. \tag{25}
\]
Here \(Q_\pi = Q(\theta)\) is the *prior* precision (the \(Q\) inside
\(Q_X = Q + A^\top D A\)), available from the prior GMRF.

**** Step 5 — Gauss–Hermite reduces the expected loglik to a \(p\)-dim quadratic

The likelihood depends on \(\psi\) only through \(\eta = A\psi\), and
conditionally factorizes, so the expected \(-\)loglik is a sum of *one-dimensional*
expectations over each \(\eta_i\) under its Gaussian marginal:
\[
\mathbb E_{\psi}\big[-\log \pi(y\mid\psi)\big]
= -\sum_i \mathbb E_{\eta_i}\big[\log \pi(y_i\mid\eta_i)\big],
\qquad \eta_i \sim \mathcal N\!\big((A\psi_1)_i,\; S_i^2\big),
\]
where the marginal predictor std is **independent of \(\lambda\)** (it depends
only on the fixed GA):
\[
S_i = \sqrt{(A^\top Q_0^{-1} A)_{ii}}\,.
\]
Each 1-D expectation is an \(m_g\)-node Gauss–Hermite quadrature (\(m_g \approx
5\)–\(11\)). Taylor-expanding the GH sum to second order *in \(\lambda\)* around
\(\lambda = 0\) (node value of the mean predictor at \(\lambda=0\) is
\(A_{i\cdot}\psi_0\)) gives per-datum linear/quadratic coefficients:
\[
B_i = \sum_{r} \frac{w_r x_r}{S_i}\,\log\pi\!\big(y_i \mid x_r S_i + A_{i\cdot}\psi_0\big),\qquad
C_i = \sum_{r} \frac{w_r (x_r^2 - 1)}{S_i^2}\,\log\pi\!\big(y_i \mid x_r S_i + A_{i\cdot}\psi_0\big).
\]
\(B_i, C_i\) are the 1st/2nd derivatives of datum \(i\)'s expected loglik w.r.t.
the mean of \(\eta_i\) (Stein-type identities for Gaussian quadrature). With
\(M_A := A\,Q_{0,I}^{-1}\) (an \(n\times p\) matrix), the expected-loglik term
becomes the \(p\)-dim quadratic
\[
\text{const} + (M_A^\top B)^\top \lambda + \tfrac12\,\lambda^\top \big(M_A^\top \operatorname{diag}(C)\, M_A\big)\,\lambda . \tag{18}
\]
Add the prior-penalty quadratic from Step 4 and you get a single \(p\times p\)
quadratic in \(\lambda\) whose minimizer is one small linear solve — *no inner
Newton over \(m\)*.

#+begin_quote
*Closed form for Poisson/Gamma (skip quadrature).* For a log-link count
likelihood the Gaussian expectation is analytic via the log-normal MGF
\(\mathbb E[e^{\eta_i}] = \exp(\text{mean}_i + \tfrac12 \text{var}_i)\). For Poisson:
\[
\mathbb E_\psi[-\log\pi(\psi\mid y)] = \sum_i\Big( \exp\!\big(A_{i\cdot}\psi_1 + \tfrac{S_i^2}{2}\big) - y_i\,A_{i\cdot}\psi_1 \Big) + \text{const}.
\]
The variance inflation \(S_i^2/2\) is *exactly* the term that makes
\(\mathbb E[e^{\eta_i}] > e^{\text{mode}}\) — i.e. the mechanism that pushes the
mean off the mode for log-link counts. In Latte the relevant per-family
derivative machinery already exists (Poisson/Gamma via log-normal MGF in
~loghessian_derivatives.jl~), so these can use the closed form; Bernoulli/Binomial
fall back to Gauss–Hermite. **Gaussian likelihood is a no-op**: the GA mode is
exact, \(\mu^* = \mu\), skip VBC entirely.
#+end_quote

**** Step 6 — feed \(\mu^*\) into the marginals, leave \(\theta\) untouched

The corrected mean \(\mu^*(\theta) = \mu(\theta) + M\lambda^*(\theta)\) replaces
\(\mu(\theta)\) in:
- the *latent* conditional marginal (variance unchanged):
  \[
  \pĩ(\psi_j\mid\theta,y) \approx \mathcal N\!\big(\mu^*_j(\theta),\; (Q_0^{-1})_{jj}\big), \tag{27}
  \]
- and, via \(\eta = A\psi\), the *predictor* conditional marginal
  \(\eta_j\mid\theta,y \sim \mathcal N\!\big((A\mu^*)_j,\; \sigma_j^2(\theta)\big)\)
  with \(\sigma_j^2 = \sum_{i,l} A_{ji}A_{jl} C_{il}\) from the selected inverse
  \(C\) (eq. 17).

Everything else in the INLA loop is *unchanged*. In particular, the
hyperparameter posterior is **kept on the GA mode**:
\[
\pĩ(\theta\mid y) \propto \frac{\pi(\psi,\theta,y)}{\pi_G(\psi\mid\theta,y)}\bigg|_{\psi=\psi_0}, \tag{28}
\]
i.e. evaluated at \(\psi_0 = \mu(\theta)\), *not* \(\mu^*\). VBC corrects only the
conditional *mean* that enters the marginal builders and the linear-combination
predictor path; it does **not** touch the \(\theta\)-weights. (In Latte:
~hyperparameter_logpdf~ at ~mode_finding.jl:172~ stays exactly as is.) The final
\(\theta\)-mixture then gives the skew of each marginal:
\[
\pĩ(\psi_j\mid y) = \sum_{k=1}^K \pĩ_{\text{VBC}}(\psi_j\mid\theta_k,y)\,\pĩ(\theta_k\mid y)\,\Delta_k .
\]

*** How VBC stays efficient

The whole point is that VBC adds *negligible* cost on top of the GA you already
compute. Overall complexity is \(\mathcal O(m p^2)\), dominated by the GA's own
Cholesky and selected inverse, with \(p \in \{2,4,\dots\}\) independent of \(m,n\).
What gets reused vs. what must never be densified:

*Reuse (no new factorizations):*
- *The GA factorization of \(Q_0 = Q + A^\top D A\)* — already computed by the
  mode finder. Factor **once**, back-substitute \(p\) times.
- *The \(p\) propagation columns* \(M = Q_{0,I}^{-1}\) — \(p\) sparse solves
  \(Q_0 x = e_j\) against that existing factor. In Latte this is exactly the
  ~conditional_column(ga, i)~ primitive (~laplace_cache.jl:205~, \(=Q^{-1}[:,i]\)
  via one back-solve).
- *The diagonal \(S_i^2 = (A^\top Q_0^{-1} A)_{ii}\)* — the *same* selected
  inverse \(C\) (Takahashi recursion from \(Q_0 = LL^\top\)) that already supplies
  the GA marginal variances \((Q_0^{-1})_{jj}\) (kept as the VBC variance) *also*
  supplies these predictor-std diagonals. The literature proves all needed
  \(C_{il}\) entries are *in-graph* (\(i \sim_{G_X} l \Rightarrow A_{ji}A_{jl}\neq
  0\)), so the \(A^\top D A\) sparsity pattern already carries them. In Latte:
  ~selected_covariance~ (~selinv.jl:30~) and ~lincomb_variance~ /
  ~linear_predictor_marginals~ (note: prefer ~linear_predictor_marginals~ for
  *constrained* models — ~lincomb_variance~ skips the Woodbury constraint
  correction).
- *The \(\theta\)-grid / CCD mixing* (\(\Delta_k\) weights) — entirely untouched.

*Cheap additions only:*
- one \(\mathcal O(n\cdot m_g)\) pass to build \(B,C\) (or the closed form for
  Poisson/Gamma), and
- one \(p\times p\) linear solve for \(\lambda^*\) (the KLD prior penalty
  \(Q_\pi M\) is sparse·(\(m\times p\)) \(= m\times p\), precomputed once).

*Densification traps (the explicit anti-patterns):*
- *Never form \(Q_0^{-1}\) densely* — only the \(p\) columns of \(M\) plus the
  selected-inverse diagonal are needed. Forming it turns \(\mathcal O(mp^2)\) into
  \(\mathcal O(m^3)\).
- *Never form \(\operatorname{Cov}(\eta) = A Q_0^{-1} A^\top\) densely* (\(n\times
  n\)) — only its diagonal is ever required.
- *Never optimize the \(m\)-vector \(\delta\) or the \(n\)-vector \(\eta\)
  directly* — always the \(p\)-vector \(\lambda\).
- *Never refactor \(Q_0\)* — reuse the GA factor for both the mode solve and the
  \(p\) propagation solves.
- *Keep \(Q_\pi\) sparse*, and recall the shared-covariance KLD has its log-det
  and trace terms cancel — *do not compute any determinant* for the correction.

#+begin_quote
*Where this lands in Latte.* VBC is per-\(\theta\) (M, \(S_i\), \(L\) are all
\(\theta\)-dependent) and slots into ~evaluate_at_grid_point~
(~exploration/utils.jl:44~) between ~ga = gaussian_approximation(...)~ (line 74)
and the ~marginalize(...)~ call (lines 90–95): compute \(\lambda^*\), form
\(\mu^* = \text{mean}(ga) + M\lambda^*\), and pass it downstream so the marginal
builders (~gaussian_marginal.jl:25~, and the predictor/lincomb path) use \(\mu^*\)
instead of \(\text{mean}(ga)\). A "compute \(M/\lambda\) once at \(\hat\theta\) and
reuse across the grid" shortcut is *off-spec* (the method specifies per-\(\theta\)
VBC) and is an accuracy trade-off to be escalated, not silently adopted.
#+end_quote

* Part II --- Design
** Executive summary

This is the committed, implementation-ready design. It promotes the *compact*
latent formulation (latent = base components only, ~η = Aψ~, GA Hessian
~Q + AᵀDA~) plus a low-rank *Variational Bayes Correction* (VBC) of the
conditional mean to the **default** INLA path, while keeping the classic
*augmented* path (explicit ~η~ nodes + per-node Simplified Laplace) fully
available as an explicit opt-in. No fork, no duplicated pipeline.

The design resolves the two candidate proposals by committing to:

1. *One construction switch* — the existing ~augment~ flag — whose DSL/front-door
   default flips to ~false~ (compact). (From Candidate A: do NOT add an
   ~InferenceMode~ enum; it duplicates state already carried by ~augment~ +
   ~augmentation_info~ and invites desync. Candidate B's enum is rejected as
   surplus surface.)
2. *One new ~MarginalApproximation~ subtype* — ~VBCMarginal~ — selected through
   the existing ~latent_marginalization_method~ kwarg, with a one-line resolver
   ~default_marginalization(model)~ keying off ~augmentation_info~. (Candidate A's
   minimal switch story.)
3. *μ\* delivered by explicit threading of a plain vector* — NOT a
   ~MeanCorrectedGMRF~ wrapper (an earlier draft's keystone, now *rejected*). The
   GA is a stateful, pooled, *versioned* ~WorkspaceGMRF~ whose behaviour-critical
   API is dispatched on its CONCRETE type: ~mean~, ~var~, ~logdetcov~, ~logpdf~
   (+ its AD ~rrule~), ~_rand!~, and — decisively — the predictor-variance
   constraint correction ~_apply_lpm_constraint_correction!(…, ::WorkspaceGMRF)~.
   A plain ~AbstractGMRF~ wrapper would silently miss those (MethodError, or a
   dense/wrong generic fallback that drops the rw1 sum-to-zero Woodbury
   correction — the *exact* trap §6 forbids), and ~mean(::WorkspaceGMRF)~ is
   itself a computed KKT-projected quantity, not a free field. So VBC returns μ\*
   as a ~Vector{Float64}~ and threads it: base marginals are built directly as
   ~Normal(μ*[j], σ_j)~ with σ from the *real* GA's selected inverse; the
   predictor/fitted marginals take their *variance* from
   ~linear_predictor_marginals(ga, obs_lik)~ on the genuine ~WorkspaceGMRF~
   (constraint-correct) and their *mean* from ~A·μ\*~. All stateful,
   constraint-aware machinery stays on the real GA; only the location is swapped,
   as a value. See §"μ\* delivery (explicit threading)".
4. *The correction kernel in one new file* ~src/laplace/vbc.jl~, reusing the GA's
   single factorization: ~p~ ~conditional_column~ back-solves for the propagation
   matrix ~M~, the same selected inverse for variances and predictor std,
   closed-form (Poisson/Gamma) or ~p~-dim Gauss–Hermite for ~λ~, and — for
   constrained components (rw1 sum-to-zero) — the rank-~p~ shift ~Mλ~ *projected
   onto the constraint tangent space* (the same Woodbury projection the
   forward-mode IFT uses), so μ\* stays constraint-consistent. Cost ~O(m p²)~,
   ~p~ independent of ~m,n~.
5. *Scope — read this.* VBC corrects only the per-θ conditional *mean*; the
   conditional stays Gaussian, so all marginal skew comes from the θ-mixture. That
   is the right trade for the data-rich regime VBC targets (near-Gaussian
   conditionals + integrated θ), but it does NOT reproduce genuine *conditional*
   skew at a fixed / sharply-concentrated θ with a strongly non-Gaussian
   likelihood. There, use the augmented+SLA mode (full per-node skew) — or, as a
   *secondary, non-blocking* goal, a compact+SLA mode that keeps component skew at
   the better conditioning, once the transformed-likelihood SLA fallback is fixed.
   See §"Scope, limitations & mode choice".

Requirements map: §3 (Coexistence with augmentation) → R1; §2/§9 (named
types/files/signatures, minimal surface) → R2; §6 (Densification audit) → R3;
§5 (the six dimensions) → R4. §7 is the per-θ loop; §10 the phased plan;
§"Scope, limitations & mode choice" the conditional-skew trade-off + 3-mode space.

** The rationale this design encodes (skeleton only)

Full equation-level derivation lives in the prose explainer. Design-relevant
skeleton:

- *Augmented (legacy):* latent ~X = [η; x_base]~, ~η ≈ A x_base~ pinned by
  ~Q_η = 1e6·I~. Payoff: diagonal likelihood ⇒ cheap uniform per-node SLA skew.
  Cost: ~+n~ near-singular nodes, cond ~~1e13~, unsafe warm-start.
- *Compact (new default):* latent ~ψ = x_base~, ~η = Aψ~, GA ~Q_X = Q + AᵀDA~ of
  size ~m~, well-conditioned. The likelihood is no longer diagonal in ~ψ~ ⇒ the
  per-node SLA premise is gone ⇒ conditional marginals are Gaussian *on the mode*
  ~N(μ_j,(Q_X⁻¹)_jj)~; all skew comes only from the θ-mixture.
- *The gap:* the GA mode ~μ(θ)~ is biased for the mean under skewed likelihoods.
  The dropped SLA used to supply that location fix (~γ₁~).
- *VBC is the replacement:* recover the conditional mean by a rank-~p~ RHS
  perturbation ~μ*(θ) = μ(θ) + M λ*(θ)~, keep the GA variance, leave the
  θ-weights on the mode. One extra ~p~-dim solve per θ.

**** μ\* delivery — explicit threading, *not* a GMRF wrapper

VBC produces μ\* as a plain ~Vector{Float64}~ and delivers it to the consumers *as
a value*. An earlier draft wrapped the GA in a ~MeanCorrectedGMRF <: AbstractGMRF~
that overrode ~mean~; that is *unsound* and is dropped. The GA is a stateful,
pooled, *versioned* ~WorkspaceGMRF~ whose behaviour-critical API is dispatched on
its CONCRETE type — ~logpdf~ (+ its AD ~rrule~), ~var~, ~logdetcov~, ~_rand!~,
and — decisively — the predictor-variance constraint correction
~_apply_lpm_constraint_correction!(…, ::WorkspaceGMRF)~ — none of which a wrapper
type can satisfy (it gets a MethodError, or a silent dense/wrong generic fallback
that *drops the rw1 sum-to-zero Woodbury correction*, the exact §6 trap). And
~mean(::WorkspaceGMRF)~ is itself a *computed* KKT-projected quantity, not a free
field, so "override mean" is not even well-defined for constrained models.

A vector cannot be dispatched on, so there is *nothing to miss*: the genuine
~WorkspaceGMRF~ remains the only GMRF in play. μ\* reaches its consumers through
three one-line seams — the honest price of the workspace's concrete-typed surface:

1. *Base-component marginals* — built straight from μ\* and the GA's selected-
   inverse std (see ~_marginalize_impl(::VBCMarginal)~ below; μ\* arrives via the
   ~mean_override~ kwarg, a defaulted no-op for every other method).
2. *Predictor / fitted marginals* — *variance* from the real GA (constraint-
   correct), *mean* from ~A·μ\*~:
   #+begin_src julia
   μ_η, v_η, _ = linear_predictor_marginals(ga, obs_lik)  # constraint-correct VAR, real ga
   η_marginals = Normal.(obs_lik.design_matrix * μ_star, sqrt.(v_η))  # corrected MEAN = A·μ*
   #+end_src
   The only added work is the sparse ~A·μ\*~ matvec; the expensive (constraint-
   corrected) variance is the GA's own. (Gaussian at fixed θ; the θ-mixture
   supplies marginal skew.)
3. *Accumulators* — receive μ\* via the ~x_star_vbc~ field of the
   ~evaluate_at_grid_point~ NamedTuple (§7) for the η location; the ~ga~ they get
   is the *unwrapped* ~WorkspaceGMRF~, so every ~::WorkspaceGMRF~-typed call
   (logpdf, its rrule, the LPM constraint correction) keeps working verbatim.

"Variance unchanged" is then preserved not by a wrapper invariant but because μ\*
is *never* fed anywhere a covariance is derived — σ always comes from the real GA.
The price (three explicit touch-points vs one wrapper) is real but each is a single
line and independently testable; the wrapper's apparent "single source of truth"
was an illusion that broke the workspace's dispatch contract.

**** The correction kernel ~vbc_correction~

#+begin_src julia
"""
    vbc_correction(ga, obs_lik, prior_gmrf, I; n_gh = 7)
        -> (μ_star::Vector{Float64}, λ::Vector{Float64})

One per-θ Variational Bayes mean correction. `I` is the p-vector of hub indices.
Returns μ* = mean(ga) + M λ (M = the p columns Q_X⁻¹[:,I]); variance untouched.
Cost O(m p²): p back-solves against the EXISTING GA factor + an O(n·n_gh)
(or O(n) closed-form) coefficient pass + one p×p solve. Never densifies Q_X⁻¹.
"""
function vbc_correction(ga, obs_lik, prior_gmrf, I::AbstractVector{<:Integer}; n_gh::Int = 7)
    A     = obs_lik.design_matrix                       # n×m sparse  (verified field)
    base  = obs_lik.base_likelihood                     # per-obs family (verified field)
    μ0    = mean(ga)                                     # GA mode ψ₀
    p     = length(I)

    # (1) p propagation columns  M = Q_X⁻¹[:,I] — reuse the GA factor, p back-solves.
    #     conditional_column carries the WorkspaceGMRF constraint (Woodbury) correction,
    #     so A·Σ_c = 0 ⇒ M's columns lie in the constraint tangent ⇒ μ* = μ0 + Mλ stays
    #     constraint-consistent automatically (μ0 is already the KKT-projected mean).
    #     Do NOT bypass conditional_column with a raw factor solve, or μ* drifts off Aψ=e.
    lsc = GaussianMarkovRandomFields.linsolve_cache(ga)
    M   = reduce(hcat, (conditional_column(ga, j, lsc) for j in I))   # m×p, constraint-correct

    # (2) predictor std S_i = √((A Q_X⁻¹ Aᵀ)_ii) and predictor mean η0 — via the
    #     constraint-correct selected-inverse path, NEVER a dense A Q⁻¹ Aᵀ. §6.
    η0, S = _vbc_predictor_moments(ga, obs_lik)         # both length-n

    # (3) per-obs linear/quadratic coeffs of the expected −loglik about λ=0
    Bc, Cc = _vbc_coefficients(base, obs_lik, η0, S; n_gh = n_gh)   # length-n each

    # (4) assemble & solve the p×p quadratic (prior penalty from §"step 4"):
    M_A = A * M                                          # n×p
    Qπ  = precision_matrix(prior_gmrf)                  # sparse m×m (no determinant)
    QπM = Qπ * M                                         # m×p
    Hλ  = M_A' * (Cc .* M_A) .+ M' * QπM                # p×p, SPD for log-concave lik
    gλ  = M_A' * Bc          .+ (QπM' * μ0)             # p
    λ   = -(Symmetric(Hλ) \ gλ)
    return (μ0 .+ M * λ, λ)
end
#+end_src

**** Predictor moments helper (constraint-correctness baked in)

#+begin_src julia
# η0 = A·μ0 + offset and S_i = √((A Q_X⁻¹ Aᵀ)_ii). Reuses GMRFs'
# linear_predictor_marginals, which applies the Woodbury constraint correction
# (the documented gap that lincomb_variance skips, linear_combinations.jl:14-20).
function _vbc_predictor_moments(ga, obs_lik)
    μ_η, v_η, _eta_lik = GaussianMarkovRandomFields.linear_predictor_marginals(ga, obs_lik)

end
#+end_src

DECISION (resolves the candidates' S_i tension): always route S_i through
~linear_predictor_marginals~ rather than a hand-rolled ~selected_covariance~
contraction. It is constraint-correct for both constrained and unconstrained GAs,
it is the exact path the accumulators already trust, and it returns η0 and S in
one call. The hand-rolled selinv contraction is kept only as a documented
fallback for backends where ~linear_predictor_marginals~ is undefined.

**** Coefficient passes — closed form vs Gauss–Hermite (R4d fast paths)

#+begin_src julia
# Gaussian likelihood: GA mode exact ⇒ VBC is a no-op. Belt-and-braces; the caller
# also short-circuits before ever wrapping (see _is_vbc_correctable, §3).
_vbc_coefficients(::NormalLikelihood, args...; kwargs...) = (Float64[], Float64[])

# Poisson/Gamma log-link: closed form via log-normal MGF E[e^η] = exp(mean + ½var).
# B_i, C_i = 1st/2nd derivs of Σ_i (E[e^{η_i}] − y_i·mean_i) w.r.t. mean_i at η0_i.
function _vbc_coefficients(::Union{PoissonLikelihood, GammaLikelihood}, obs_lik, η0, S; kwargs...)
    Eexp = @. exp(η0 + S^2 / 2)
    y    = _vbc_response(obs_lik)            # observed counts/responses
    return (Eexp .- y, Eexp)                 # (B, C); C ≥ 0
end

# Bernoulli/Binomial (and any non-MGF family): n_gh-node Gauss–Hermite over η_i.
function _vbc_coefficients(::ObservationLikelihood, obs_lik, η0, S; n_gh::Int)
    x, w = gausshermite_nodes(n_gh)          # reuse the GH cache, laplace_cache.jl:6
    B = similar(η0); C = similar(η0)
    @inbounds for i in eachindex(η0)
        bi = 0.0; ci = 0.0; si = S[i]
        for r in eachindex(x)
            ℓ = pointwise_logpdf_at(obs_lik, i, x[r] * si + η0[i])   # log π(y_i | η)
            bi += (w[r] * x[r] / si) * ℓ
            ci += (w[r] * (x[r]^2 - 1) / si^2) * ℓ
        end
        B[i] = -bi                            # derivatives of −loglik
        C[i] = max(-ci, 0.0)                  # clamp ≥ 0 (guards a non-convex outlier)
    end
    return (B, C)
end
#+end_src

The Poisson/Gamma branch reuses the per-family log-normal-MGF identities already
in ~src/model/loghessian_derivatives.jl~. The GH branch needs a scalar per-obs
log-density ~pointwise_logpdf_at(obs_lik, i, η)~, a one-element evaluation of the
base family's log-density — no new likelihood code.

** The six dimensions VBC changes/enables (R4)

*** (a) Latent field — augmented ↔ compact via the ~augment~ flag

Served entirely by switch 1. Compact ⇒ ctor pass-through (~:226~),
~augmentation_info === nothing~, latent = base. Augmented ⇒ ctor wraps in
~AugmentedLatentModel~ (~:262~) and sets ~AugmentationInfo~ (~:270~). The
~latent_layout~ / ~latent_groups~ are populated identically (compact just lacks
the leading η range), so named-block lookups, accumulators, and
~linear_combinations~ keep working in both modes. No new machinery.

*** (b) GA — diagonal (augmented) vs ~Q + AᵀDA~ (compact)

Untouched by VBC. ~gaussian_approximation~ already builds the right GA per
formulation. VBC CONSUMES the compact GA's factorization; it never alters how the
GA is built. The DSL non-augmented fast path pre-unions ~Q_base~ with the ~AᵀA~
boolean pattern (~_bool_AtA_pattern~, ~adapter.jl:298~) so the workspace symbolic
factor covers ~Q+AᵀDA~ — VBC's ~p~ back-solves and selected inverse run against
exactly that factor.

*** (c) The mean correction — rank-~p~ VBC: WHERE, index set, selinv reuse

*WHERE (per-θ):* inside ~evaluate_at_grid_point~ (~exploration/utils.jl:44~),
between ~ga = gaussian_approximation(...)~ (line 74) and ~marginalize(...)~ (line
90). VBC is per-θ because M, S_i, the factor are all θ-dependent; this function is
already called per grid point (~inla_grid.jl:130~, ~ccd.jl:184~). The detailed
hook is §7.

*INDEX SET I for OUR models* (~resolve_vbc_indices~ → ~latent_index_set_for_vbc~):

*** (d) Marginals — Gaussian base + VBC mean + predictor lincombs; SLA replacement

- *Base latent marginals:* ~_marginalize_impl(::VBCMarginal)~ emits
  ~Normal(μ*_j, σ_j)~ — same shape as ~GaussianMarginal~, mean from ~μ*~ (threaded
  in as a vector via ~mean_override~), variance ~σ_j~ from the GA selected-inverse
  diagonal (unchanged). This IS the per-node-SLA replacement: SLA's location fix
  (~γ₁~) is now supplied *jointly* by ~Mλ~; the *skew* comes only from the
  θ-mixture (~create_weighted_mixtures~, unchanged).
- *Predictor marginals* η = Aψ: in compact mode the ~inla~ augmentation split
  (~inference.jl:170–176~) does NOT fire, so predictor marginals are derived
  *explicitly*: *variance* from ~linear_predictor_marginals(ga, obs_lik)~ on the
  *real, unwrapped* GA (the constraint-correct ~v_η~), *mean* from ~A·μ*~ (one
  sparse matvec). The accumulators (DIC/WAIC/CPO, ~accumulator_core.jl:120~) take
  μ* via the ~x_star_vbc~ NamedTuple field for the η location; the ~ga~ they
  receive is unwrapped, so their ~::WorkspaceGMRF~ logpdf/rrule paths are untouched.
  The public ~INLAResult.linear_predictor_marginals~ field
  (~augmentation_info === nothing~) is populated the same way:
  ~Normal.(obs_lik.design_matrix * μ*, sqrt.(v_η))~.
- *On-demand lincombs:* a linear combination ~aᵀψ~ takes its *mean* from ~aᵀμ*~ and
  its *variance* from ~linear_predictor_marginals~ / the constraint-correct
  selected inverse (NOT ~lincomb_variance~, which skips the Woodbury correction —
  §6 trap). So ~linear_combinations~ gains a mean-vector argument (defaulting to
  ~mean(ga)~) rather than wrapping the GA; pass μ* there. Cache λ (or μ*) per
  integration point on the exploration result to avoid recomputing
  ~vbc_correction~ in the lincomb path (negligible length-~p~ / length-~m~ memory).

*** (e) The hyperparameter loop — VBC per-θ; π̃(θ|y) stays on the GA mode

- *Per-θ* (on-spec). A "compute M/λ once at θ̂ and reuse across the grid" variant
  is OFF-SPEC (M, S_i, factor are θ-dependent) and an accuracy trade-off to
  ESCALATE, not adopt silently.
- *θ-weights untouched.* ~hyperparameter_logpdf~ (~mode_finding.jl:172~) — the
  π̃(θ|y) ratio evaluated at the GA mode ~x_star = mean(ga)~ — is NOT changed
  (eq 28). The hook (§7) wraps ~ga~ AFTER ~log_density~ is computed, so the
  un-wrapped mode feeds the weights and the wrapped μ* feeds only the marginals /
  predictors. ~find_hyperparameter_mode~, the exploration/CCD/grid code, and
  ~create_weighted_mixtures~ are all unchanged.

*** (f) Warm-start safety unlocked by compact conditioning

The compact ~Q+AᵀDA~ has no ~1e6~ block (well-conditioned, ~m~-sized), so the
existing ~x0~ warm-start seam (~evaluate_at_grid_point(...; x0)~ at ~utils.jl:46/74~,
and ~_reconstruct_ga~ which currently passes NO ~x0~) becomes safe to use
aggressively: seed each grid point's Newton from a neighbour's GA mode. CRITICAL:
warm-start from the *uncorrected* mode ~μ(θ)~ (the Newton target / ~x_star~), NOT
μ* — VBC is a post-GA correction and must never feed back into the GA solve. This
is a follow-on optimization (§10 phase 4), gated on ~augmentation_info === nothing~;

** Scope, limitations & mode choice (the conditional-skew trade-off)

*** What VBC does, and does *not*, do

VBC corrects the per-θ conditional *mean* and nothing else. The per-θ conditional
marginal stays *Gaussian* (variance = the GA selected-inverse diagonal), so **all
skewness in a final marginal comes from the θ-mixture** (eq 12) — never from a
per-node conditional-skew correction. This is a trade the source paper makes
explicitly: dropping the augmentation loses the unified per-node SLA, and VBC is
introduced "precisely to recover the *mean*"; the paper even considers and
*rejects* a skew-normal-copula route as "essentially negating the computational
benefit."

Consequence to keep front of mind: at a *fixed* θ (or a sharply concentrated
θ-posterior, where the mixture is effectively a point mass) with a genuinely
non-Gaussian likelihood, compact+VBC reports a *mean-correct Gaussian* and **misses
the real conditional skew** — an accuracy loss versus augmented+SLA, which captures
it via ~γ₃~.

*** When this is fine vs when it bites

- *Fine (VBC's design target):* data-rich models with θ integrated. More data per
  latent node ⇒ near-Gaussian conditionals (CLT) ⇒ the dropped skew is genuinely
  small, and the θ-mixture supplies what remains — this is where the paper's "mean
  accuracy on par with MCMC" holds. Predictor marginals are near-Gaussian
  regardless (≤0.25% skew measured on Paraná), so they lose ~nothing.
- *Bites:* few effective observations per node + strongly non-Gaussian likelihood
  (low-count Poisson/Gamma effects), *especially* at fixed/concentrated θ where the
  mixture cannot recover the conditional skew.

*** Three modes, not two

| mode | mean | component skew | predictor skew | conditioning | speed |
|------+------+----------------+----------------+--------------+-------|
| augmented + SLA (legacy)  | SLA ~γ₁~ | yes (SLA)     | yes (per-node) | bad (~1e13~) | slow    |
| *compact + VBC* (default) | VBC      | no (Gaussian) | no (mixture)   | good (~4e8~) | fastest |
| compact + SLA (secondary) | SLA ~γ₁~ | yes (SLA)     | no (mixture)   | good (~4e8~) | medium  |

- *Default = compact + VBC* for the common data-rich / θ-integrated case.
- *Need conditional skew today?* use *augmented + SLA* (~augment=true~ +
  ~SimplifiedLaplace()~) — kept fully intact (R1). This is the supported answer now.
- *compact + SLA* would be strictly better than augmented+SLA for skew-needing
  cases (same component skew, better conditioning, faster LA — losing only the
  ~Gaussian~ predictor skew). It is a *SECONDARY, NON-BLOCKING* goal: it needs the
  transformed-likelihood SLA fallback fixed first — the 2×2 experiment showed
  today's compact+SLA both *slower* and *less accurate* (KS 0.133 vs 0.107), i.e.
  the fallback adds wrong skew (the augmentation-shadow bug resurfacing). Nice if it
  lands and is fast; fine if it doesn't land soon. NB: VBC and component-SLA are
  *alternative* mean routes — you would not run both (both correct the mean).

*** Not a silent footgun (required behaviour)

Since VBC is the default, the implementation MUST NOT silently flatten skew where it
matters: (1) document the above; (2) keep ~augment=true~ + ~SimplifiedLaplace()~ one
switch away; (3) emit a diagnostic/warning in the danger regime — very low
observations-per-latent-node, or a fixed-θ / single-point request — pointing at the
augmented mode. Part I is framed accordingly: VBC is a *mean* fix, not a full skew
approximation.

** Densification audit (R3 — every trap + how avoided)

Per-θ overhead is ~O(m p²)~, strictly dominated by the GA's own Cholesky +
selected inverse that ~evaluate_at_grid_point~ ALREADY computes.

| Quantity | Naïve dense form | Trap | How VBC avoids it |
|----------+------------------+------+-------------------|
| ~Q_X = Q+AᵀDA~ | — | — | already built by ~gaussian_approximation~; one sparse Cholesky; D diagonal so ~AᵀDA~ sparse |
| ~M = Q_X⁻¹[:,I]~ (m×p) | full ~Q_X⁻¹~ | O(m³) | ~p~ back-solves ~conditional_column(ga, j, lsc)~ against the EXISTING factor; only ~p~ columns |
| GA marginal var ~(Q_X⁻¹)_jj~ | dense inverse | O(m³) | ~selected_covariance(ga)~ Takahashi selected inverse — already computed for the marginals; VBC keeps it as σ |
| ~S_i² = (A Q_X⁻¹ Aᵀ)_ii~ | n×n ~Cov(η)~ | O(n²)+O(m³) | ~linear_predictor_marginals(ga, obs_lik)~ → only the diagonal ~v_η~; all needed entries in-graph on the ~AᵀDA~ pattern; constraint-correct |
| mean shift δ | optimize m-vector | O(m) unknowns | optimize the ~p~-vector ~λ~; ~p ∈ {1,2,4}~ independent of m,n |
| ~B,C~ coeffs | inner Newton over m | O(m·iters) | closed form Poisson/Gamma (~exp(η0+S²/2)~, ~loghessian_derivatives.jl:80–131~) or one O(n·n_gh) GH pass; NO inner Newton |
| ~M_A=A·M~ (n×p), ~Q_πM~ (m×p) | — | — | sparse·(m×p): the only new dense allocs, O(p·max(m,n)) |
| KLD / normalizer | log-det of ~Q_X~,~Q_π~ | O(m^{3/2}) | shared-covariance Gaussian KLD ⇒ log-det & trace CANCEL; only the Mahalanobis ~½ψ*ᵀQ_πψ*~ remains; NO determinant computed |
| ~λ~ solve | — | — | one ~p×p~ ~Symmetric(Hλ)\gλ~ |

*Explicit anti-patterns the code must NOT do* (assert/comment in ~vbc.jl~):
- Never ~inv(Q_X)~ / ~Matrix(Q_X⁻¹)~ — only ~p~ ~conditional_column~ solves + the
  selected-inverse diagonal.
- Never ~A * Q_X⁻¹ * A'~ as a dense ~n×n~ — only its diagonal via
  ~linear_predictor_marginals~.
- Never refactor ~Q_X~ for the VBC solves — reuse the GA factor (~linsolve_cache(ga)~).
- Never a determinant in the KLD.
- *Constraint trap:* for constrained models do NOT use ~lincomb_variance~ for S_i
  / predictor variance — it skips the Woodbury constraint correction (documented at
  ~linear_combinations.jl:14–20~). Use ~linear_predictor_marginals~ (as the
  accumulators do).

** Fast-path short-circuits

- *Gaussian likelihood ⇒ VBC no-op.* ~_is_vbc_correctable~ returns false; the
  hook never wraps; SPDEtoy Gaussian preserves its observed 1.5× speedup.
- *Poisson/Gamma ⇒ closed form*, reusing ~loghessian_derivatives.jl~'s log-normal
  MGF; the Paraná Gamma benchmark hits this path with ~p ≈ 1–4~.
- *Bernoulli/Binomial ⇒ p-dim Gauss–Hermite* (~n_gh ≈ 7~), one O(n·n_gh) pass.

  Test: count factorizations on the path == the one ~CPT-GA~ already performs.
- *Selected-inverse reuse:* assert the marginal variance ~σ_j~ reported by
  ~CPT-VBC~ is byte-identical to ~CPT-GA~'s ~std(ga)~ (VBC touches mean only).
- *No determinant in the KLD:* assert the shared-covariance KLD path computes no
  ~logdet~/~det~ of ~Q_X~ or ~Q_π~ (the log-det and trace cancel; only the
  Mahalanobis term survives). Test: the correction value is invariant to scaling
  the (cancelled) determinant terms.

*** C. CORRECTNESS

**** C1. VBC mean matches a brute-force full-VB reference

- *Reference:* on a small compact Poisson/Gamma LGM (~m~ ≈ 10–30 so a dense solve
  is tractable), compute the *full* variational mean correction by directly
  minimizing ~E_{ψ~N(ψ₀+δ,Q₀⁻¹)}[−log π(y|ψ)] + ½(ψ₀+δ)ᵀQ_π(ψ₀+δ)~ over the
  full ~m~-vector ~δ~ (Optim, dense). Then compute the rank-~p~ VBC ~μ*~.
- *Pass thresholds:*
  - With ~I~ = all indices (~p = m~): ~μ*~ from ~vbc_correction~ equals the full-VB
    ~ψ₀+δ~ to ~1e-8~ (the low-rank restriction is then no restriction — this
    validates the λ-solve and the coefficient passes exactly).
  - With the default low-rank ~I~ (~p ≪ m~): ~μ*~ matches the full-VB solution in
    the *hub* coordinates ~I~ to ~1e-6~ and recovers ≥ ~90%~ of the full-VB mean
    shift in the *propagated* (non-hub) coordinates (the joint-improvement
    guarantee — any reasonable ~I~ gives a similarly-improved joint mean).
  - Poisson closed-form vs Gauss–Hermite cross-check: ~_vbc_coefficients~ via the
    closed-form log-normal-MGF branch agrees with the ~n_gh~-node GH branch to
    ~1e-6~ on the same Poisson model (confirms the two paths compute the same B,C).

**** C2. The Gaussian-conditional base marginals

- ~_marginalize_impl(::VBCMarginal)~ returns ~Normal(μ*_j, σ_j)~ where ~σ_j ==
  std(ga)[j]~ exactly (variance untouched) and ~μ*_j == mean_override[j]~.
- ~mean_override~ threading tests: with ~mean_override=μ*~ the base marginals
  report μ* with σ from the *real* GA (variance bit-identical to a plain
  ~GaussianMarginal~ run); with ~mean_override=nothing~ the fallback recomputes μ*
  from the index set; crucially the ~ga~ reaching ~marginalize~ is the *unwrapped*
  ~WorkspaceGMRF~, so its ~logpdf~/~rrule~/LPM-constraint-correction dispatch is
  exercised unchanged (a wrapper would have broken these).
- *μ\* constraint-consistency:* for a constrained component (rw1 sum-to-zero),
  assert ~A·μ* ≈ e~ to tol — the ~conditional_column~ Woodbury keeps ~Mλ~ in the
  constraint tangent, so no explicit projection is needed.

**** C3. Predictor lincombs pick up μ*

- *Quantity:* the predictor mean ~η = Aψ~ obtained two ways must agree:
  (i) ~A · μ*~ computed directly, and (ii) the mean of ~linear_combinations(result,
  A)~ once μ* is threaded as its mean vector. The *variance* in both comes from
  ~linear_predictor_marginals(ga, obs_lik)~ on the real GA — assert it equals the
  plain-GA predictor variance (VBC left it untouched).
- *Pass threshold:* the two means agree to ~1e-10~ and each equals ~A·μ*~ (NOT
  ~A·μ~) — i.e. μ* propagates to the predictor path *as a value*, not via a wrapper.
- *Accumulator consistency:* DIC/WAIC/CPO accumulators take μ* via the
  ~x_star_vbc~ field for the η location while receiving the *unwrapped* GA; assert
  they (a) shift in the expected direction vs the mode-based baseline and (b) do
  not error — their ~::WorkspaceGMRF~ logpdf/rrule paths are unchanged.
- *Constraint correctness:* on a sum-to-zero-constrained component, predictor
  variance must come from ~linear_predictor_marginals~ (Woodbury-corrected), NOT
  ~lincomb_variance~ (which skips the correction). Test against a constrained model
  where the two differ measurably and assert VBC uses the corrected one.

**** C4. Coexistence — augmented mode unchanged (regression guard)

- *The hard gate for the default flip:* with ~augment=true~, ~CPT-VBC~ code present
  but dormant, the full ~inla~ output (hyperparameter marginals, latent marginals,
  predictor marginals, DIC/WAIC) must be *byte-for-byte / within solver tolerance*
  identical to current ~main~ (~AUG-SLA~). Snapshot current-main outputs on
  Scotland, Seeds, Epil, Paraná-augmented before the change; assert post-change
  equality (≤ ~1e-10~ on marginal moments).
- The VBC branch must be provably not entered when ~augmentation_info !== nothing~
  (assert the guard; assert ~ga~ is never wrapped).
- Cross-pairing errors: ~VBCMarginal~ on an augmented model throws a clear
  ~ArgumentError~ from both ~_marginalize_impl~ and ~validate_inla_inputs~; test the
  message names the fix ("construct with augment=false").

*** D. EDGE CASES

**** D1. Strong skew — VBC correction is large and right

- *Construct:* the low-count Poisson at mean count 0.3 (A2) and a Gamma with small
  shape (heavy per-conditional skew).
- *Assert:* ~‖Mλ‖∞~ is large (the mean shift is a meaningful fraction of σ); the
  shift has the correct sign (toward the heavy tail / above the mode for log-link);
  and ~CPT-VBC~ closes ≥ ~75%~ of the ~CPT-GA~→oracle mean gap (A2 threshold). This
  is the case that must move the most.

**** D2. Near-Gaussian — VBC ≈ 0 (no spurious motion)

- *Construct:* (a) a ~NormalLikelihood~ compact model (SPDEtoy); (b) a high-count
  Poisson (mean count = 50, conditionals nearly symmetric).
- *Assert:* (a) ~_is_vbc_correctable~ returns ~false~, VBC is never entered, and
  ~CPT-VBC~ marginals are *identical* to ~CPT-GA~ (the GA is exact for Gaussian, so
  μ*=μ by construction); (b) for the high-count Poisson, ~‖Mλ‖∞ / max σ < 0.02~ —
  VBC does essentially nothing and does not introduce spurious bias. This guards
  against a kernel that "corrects" where no correction is warranted.

**** D3. Ill-conditioning — the conditioning win is real and VBC is stable

- *Assert (compact conditioning):* the compact GA Hessian ~Q+AᵀDA~ condition
  number on Paraná is many orders of magnitude below the augmented
  formulation's ~~1e13~ (measure ~cond~ of both; assert compact ≤ ~1e8~ and ≥ ~1e4×~
  better than augmented). This is the structural premise of the whole design.
- *Assert (VBC numerical stability):* the ~p×p~ ~Hλ = Symmetric(M_Aᵀ diag(C) M_A +
  Mᵀ Q_π M)~ is SPD for the log-concave likelihoods (Poisson/Gamma/Bernoulli/
  Binomial) — the solve ~Hλ\gλ~ succeeds without pivoting failure; the ~C ≥ 0~
  clamp in the GH branch is exercised and keeps ~Hλ~ SPD on a deliberately
  non-convex-outlier datum.

**** D4. Mode-finding warm-start becomes SAFE under compact conditioning

- *The claim under test:* compact ~Q+AᵀDA~ (no ~1e6~ block) makes the existing
  ~x0~ warm-start seam (~evaluate_at_grid_point(...; x0)~) safe to use across the
  θ-grid, which the augmented path cannot.
- *Procedure:* run the compact Paraná θ-grid twice — (i) cold (each grid point's
  Newton from the default init), (ii) warm (each grid point seeded from a
  neighbour's *uncorrected* GA mode ~μ(θ)~, never μ*).
- *Pass thresholds:*
  - *Safety/identity:* warm and cold produce the same posterior marginals to
    solver tolerance (≤ ~1e-8~ on marginal moments) — warm-start must not change
    the answer.
  - *Convergence:* warm-start strictly reduces total Newton iterations across the
    grid (assert ≥ ~20%~ fewer), and *never* diverges or hits the iteration cap
    (the failure mode the augmented ~1e13~ conditioning would risk).
  - *Negative control:* attempting the same neighbour-seeded warm-start on the
    augmented path is either gated off or demonstrably less stable (more
    iterations / occasional cap hits) — documenting *why* warm-start is unlocked
    only by the compact formulation.
  - *No feedback bug:* assert the warm-start seed is ~μ(θ)~ (the Newton target),
    explicitly NOT μ* — a test that perturbs μ* and confirms the GA solve result
    is unchanged (μ* must never re-enter the GA).
